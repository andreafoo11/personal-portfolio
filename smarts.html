SMARTS Articulate360 Series

A Metacognition and Executive Function learning experience targeted at elementary through high school students using Aritculate360. SMARTS empowers students by helping them understand their strengths and weaknesses and teaching them critically important executive function strategies.

My role

For my current internship in a non-profit that is currently developing metacognition and executive function curriculum and learning experiences for elementary through high school students, I am developing a lot of modules and content primarily using Vyond and Articulate 360 and Storyline. Due to confidentiality reasons, I will not be showing everything that I created for SMARTS on my portfolio, but here are a few parts of it to give an idea of what I’ve been doing!


Objectives of the project

  • Boost student engagement: More connections to their lives, more interaction in activities

  • Address teacher needs: Content specific connections, lessons span more than one year, tools for differentiation

  • Effective delivery format: Reduce ability to download everything, update materials to reflect current instructional design practices

Reflection

My experience at the internship has been positive overall because everyone has been very welcoming and open to me working on any project that I am interested in. As they just started a new project and are now in the initial phase of creating learning modules, it is a very exciting time for me to be there because I had a lot of flexibility and creative freedom as an instructional design intern. In meetings, my ideas and suggestions felt like they were heard and I was able to actually help contribute to the project that will eventually help students all across the US. I also soon became the main point person when it came to creating animation videos on Vyond.

During the course of my internship, I applied a lot of theories that I learned from the online module “How People Learn”. By students at different ages learn and take in knowledge, I was able to create videos and digital portfolio samples that best suited the age group that we targeted.

One challenge that I encountered during this internship was that I wanted to work more on Articulate, but found myself assigned a lot more tasks using Vyond and other animation software. Though it was interesting as well, Articulate was something that I wanted to learn more starting out this internship. After a few weeks, I decided to ask my supervisor whether or not I could work more on Articulate tasks, and also get the company’s Articulate log in information to work on it. I felt very uncomfortable asking this, but stepping outside my comfort zone to do so paid off!

Through this internship, I saw the nuanced interactions between co-workers and learned methods of remote working that felt very different from in person work. Although I was not really able to truly integrate myself into the workplace culture since I was remote the whole semester, I truly felt very welcomed and heard by every member of the team. This changed my opinion of remote work, as I can see that I can still form real relationships through remote work. Additionally, through observing how Michael led meetings and distributed work, I saw important characteristics to have as a leader and the need to be organized, have an agenda, and clearly assign tasks to members to keep the project on track. I used to think what made a great leader was that they knew everything and did everything, but now I think that a great leader is one who knows how to create a clear structured timeline and assign tasks to team members. It is important to trust your team and trust that they will do the work that they are assigned, which they all did.
